Smart Cities: A Comprehensive Overview

What is a Smart City?
A smart city uses digital technology and data-driven approaches to enhance urban services, improve quality of life, and reduce environmental impact. The core goals are efficiency, sustainability, and citizen well-being.

Technologies Used
Smart cities rely on technologies such as Internet of Things (IoT) sensors, cloud computing, artificial intelligence, and big data analytics. These technologies allow for real-time monitoring and decision-making.

Applications
Applications of smart cities include intelligent traffic management, smart energy grids, waste management systems, and digital governance platforms that improve public service delivery.

Challenges
Smart cities face several challenges, including data privacy, cybersecurity risks, high implementation costs, and lack of technical expertise.

Future Scope
The future of smart cities involves deeper integration of AI for predictive analytics, citizen-centric design, and sustainable urban planning supported by renewable energy.
